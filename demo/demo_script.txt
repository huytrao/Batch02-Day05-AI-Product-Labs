# Demo Script — Ocean Park 1 Eats (3 minutes)

## Demo structure:
- Happy path: ~90 seconds
- Failure path: ~30 seconds  
- Q&A: ~30 seconds
- **Total: 3 minutes**

---

## HAPPY PATH (90 seconds)

### Setup (10 seconds)
"Hello. We're building a quick restaurant finder for Ocean Park 1 residents. The problem: people waste 10+ minutes checking Facebook, Google Maps, and delivery apps to find what to eat and whether it will deliver fast.

Our solution: aggregate data from multiple sources and recommend 2–3 restaurants with predicted delivery time and confidence."

### Demo (70 seconds)
1. **Open prototype** (5 seconds)
   - Show a simple page: search box + "Giao nhanh (Fast Delivery)" button
   - Explain: "This is our MVP. No code yet—just UI flow and fake data."

2. **Search** (15 seconds)
   - Click search or enter default: "Ocean Park 1, now"
   - System processes (show loading for 2–3 seconds)
   - Show results table:
     | Restaurant | ETA (min) | Confidence | Source Links |
     |---|---|---|---|
     | Bún Bò Huế Anh Đào | 35–40 | 0.92 | FB / Google / Grab ✓ |
     | Cơm Tấm Sài Gòn | 40–45 | 0.78 | Google / Shopee ✓ |

3. **Evidence** (20 seconds)
   - Click first restaurant result
   - Show evidence pop-up:
     - "Recent Facebook post (2h ago): 'Vẫn mở và giao nhanh lắm'"
     - Shopee ETA: 35–40 min (last updated 30 min ago)
     - Google: "Open now · 4.8★ · 47 reviews"
   - Explain: "We show sources so user can trust the recommendation. If confidence is low, we ask user to call first."

4. **Confirm and next step** (10 seconds)
   - User clicks "View Menu" → redirects to Shopee/Grab
   - Say: "In Day 06, we'll build the actual UI. Today, we just wanted to show the flow and validate the idea with you."

### Transition (5 seconds)
"That's the happy path. Now let me show you how we handle when things go wrong."

---

## FAILURE PATH (30 seconds)

### Setup (5 seconds)
"Scenario: what if the AI makes a wrong prediction?"

### Demo (20 seconds)
1. **Show second restaurant (Cơm Tấm Sài Gòn)** with low confidence (0.78)
   - Explain: "Here, signals conflict. Google says open but no recent Shopee update. Confidence is lower."

2. **What does the prototype do?**
   - Show message: ⚠️ "Low confidence. Recommendation based on outdated data. Call ahead?"
   - Offer: [Call] [Choose different] [See all sources]

3. **If user calls and finds it closed:**
   - User submits feedback form: "Closed"
   - System logs and updates evidence tag
   - Prototype updates next recommendation

4. **Fallback options:**
   - Alternative suggestion: Bánh Mì Anh Đông (0.88 confidence, 30–35 min)
   - Say: "We always have a backup. And the team owner tracks failures for Day 06 validation."

### Transition (5 seconds)
"That's the failure path. Ready for questions?"

---

## Q&A (30 seconds)

Prepare answers for likely questions:

1. **Q: Why not just integrate with Grab/Shopee APIs?**
   - A: Too risky and slow for Day 05. We're validating the idea first with fake data. Day 06, if this idea is strong, we plan scraping or partnership.

2. **Q: How do you handle privacy/scraping TOS?**
   - A: Right now, we use only public posts and anonymized quotes. No scraping of personal data. We'll revisit this in Day 06.

3. **Q: What if the delivery time estimate is still wrong?**
   - A: That's why we show confidence and sources. User always has the choice to confirm before ordering. We're not fully automating—just augmenting their decision.

4. **Q: How long to build this in Day 06?**
   - A: We have 6 hours. We'll focus on the core: SQLite DB + simple ranking heuristic + minimal UI. No payment, no live scraping, no complex algorithm.

---

## Key messages to hammer home:

1. **Evidence-first:** We're not building on assumptions. Every recommendation has a source.
2. **User control:** AI suggests but user decides. Failure mode is clear.
3. **Scope:** One slice only (Fast Delivery), not the full app. Fake data for MVP.
4. **Day 06 ready:** We have schema, demo script, and owner assignments to ship by end of Day 06.
